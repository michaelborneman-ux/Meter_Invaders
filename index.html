What is a Gas Meter?

A gas meter measures how much natural gas flows through it. It tracks total usage in cubic feet.

Your job as a meter reader is to record the 4-digit number shown on the dials — accurately, every time.

The 4 dials together give you the reading.
Each dial shows one digit: thousands, hundreds, tens, and units.

In the next steps you'll learn how to read each dial correctly.

Clockwise & Counter-clockwise Dials

Each dial rotates in a different direction, alternating CW and CCW from left to right.

Clockwise (CW)
Numbers increase →
Counter-clockwise (CCW)
Numbers increase ←

Always check the CW / CCW badge below each dial before reading it.

Reading the Needle

The needle always sits between two numbers. Read the lower number the needle has already passed.

This dial → read 4
Right dial shows 3
The right dial tells you exactly where the needle is:
Its digit = how far past the lower number the left needle has traveled.

Right = 1 → needle is 1/10 past the lower number
Right = 3 → needle is 3/10 past (like above — read 4)
Right = 9 → needle is 9/10 past (almost at the next — still read the lower)
Right = 0 → needle is exactly on the number — read it

Reading All 4 Dials

Read from left to right. Each dial gives one digit of the 4-digit reading.

Reading = —
Left dial = thousands  |  Right dial = units
